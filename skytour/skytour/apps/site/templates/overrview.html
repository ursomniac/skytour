Set the Cookie

To do almost anything you'll need to set the Cookie. This handles all the calculations surrounding object positions, and their relative availabilities. Most functions will let you offset the date/time of the cookie as needed.

Events/Calendar

These pages have information about:

  • Events happening over the next few days
  • Meteor Showers
  • "Close" Proximity of Planets to Each Other
  • Calendar over the following few weeks

There's also a calendar by month. Each day has:

  • a background color that reflects the current Moon phase (brighter close to Full Moon; darker close to New Moon)
  • The times of
    • sunset/sunride
    • moonrise/moonset
    • end/beginning of Astronomical Twilight
  • List of events on that date


Finding Objects to Observe

Depending on how you're planning to observe and how much planning you want to make, there are several options.

Skymap

This will show you the sky either at the date/time and observing location set in the Cookie, or right now. You can offset the date/time from the Skymap page. If you've defined a "mask" for your observing location (for trees, buildings, or other obstructions) you can make that appear as well. There's also a "simple" mode with fewer things on it that is good for printing.

Several DSOs are shown on the Skymap. These aren't ALL of the brightest or largest objects - sometimes they've been added to "fill in" a part of the sky, and sometimes popular objects aren't there to avoid clutter.





@Now and @Cookie Views

For finding which DSOs are "up" there are two pages: @Home and @Cookie which filter the DSO catalog for those that are above the horizon at the specific date/time/location. From those pages you can further filter to narrow your search.

If you have your laptop in the field, these pages are very helpful while observing!

The lists can optionally take location masking into consideration (if you've set it up), so that you don't waste time trying to find something behind your neighbor's house!


Deep Sky Objects (DSOs)

There are several ways to find DSOs. The search box in the navbar will go to a DSO detail page. The DSO Search knows about most aliases, and also about any fainter DSOs in the field of other brighter DSOs! So, both "M 46" or "NGC 2437" will get you to the right page, as well as "NGC 2438" (the planetary nebula in the same field as M 46).

Several pages have lists of DSOs (@Home, @Cookie, Constellation Detail pages, Atlas pages).
